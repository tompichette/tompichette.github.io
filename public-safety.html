Public Safety

sUAS have revolutionized the accident reconstruction space by providing a safe and efficient method of site reconstruction. Previously setting up terrestrial lidar systems and extensive photography could take hours. An sUAS provides better data in a matter of minutes.

Body cameras can only capture so much information and having a drone on site for an operation will allow a birds eye view without launching a costly helicopter. Drones provide accountability of those on site and improve the situational awareness of Command and Control elements.

Search and Rescue

Drones are a vital tool that can be rapidly deployed for Search and Rescue operations.

Utilizing visual and thermal sensors saves lives. A helicopter takes time to launch and get on site for a search. When every second matters, utilizing a drone will provide an aerial asset on site immediately. Thermal sensors can be set to alert the operator to heat signatures in the temperature ranges of human subjects allowing for fast and thorough scanning of a given area.

Emergency Management and Disaster Recovery

Having a drone on standby to respond to disasters is crucial. Drones provide a birds-eye view of the environment before you set foot into uncertain terrain, mitigating risk in a way not possible before.

Drones help to identify and locate the isolated or injured, providing critical information to make recovery faster. Drones can deliver lifesaving equipment and supplies to impossible to reach locations.
