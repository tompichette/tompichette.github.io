Distance Learning with Captive Drone

Captive Drone is now offering full online training and program development. With remote course offerings that cover everything from Part 107 to simulator-based flight training, we are ready to help get your program started.

Captive Drone works with utilities to provide the following:

  • Training and licensing of pilots to Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) standards
  • Creating Standard Operating Procedures
  • Providing equipment & software solutions
  • Managing flight details, so you never have to deal with the FAA
  • Developing Safety Protocols

Even while sheltering in place, you can take steps to get your drone program started.

Why This Program?

Our hands-on inspection experience in the electric utility industry brings a unique perspective and insight into the challenges of asset condition monitoring. This provides us with a keen awareness of the dangers and prohibitive costs associated with elevated infrastructure inspection.

Our goal is to provide solutions that drastically reduce costs of Unmanned Aerial System inspections as well as the safety risks associated with conventional inspection methods. The end result is our innovative software platform that can reduce the need for the time-consuming, costly and risky inspections of the past using pre-set drone flight plans for automated data collection.

Captive Drone provides cutting edge programs that implement the latest technology for automated inspection and top of the line inspection equipment.

Trust our expert pilots and industry insiders to work with you to develop a highly customized drone program. With real-time support and annual refresher training, you'll always have the most up-to-date program so that you can be confident of FAA compliance.

Utility Pilot Course Syllabus

Day 1

Airspace

Students will learn the ins-and-outs of the National Airspace System and how to safely navigate U.S. airspace with a drone.

Weather

Understand the impacts of weather on aircraft performance, how to look up and decipher aviation weather reports, and the restrictions imposed by weather.

Regulations

Students will learn and understand FAA regulations regarding the use of drones in the U.S. under 14 CFR part 107.

Loading and Performance

Lean the effects of loading and weather conditions on the performance of drones. Students will be able to apply the knowledge of perforance to safely and effectively plan and fly drone missions.

Day 2

Airport Operations

Students will learn how manned aircraft operate in and around airports to understand how drones fit into the complexities of traffic patterns and approach procedures.

Radio Communication Procedures

Learn how to understand aviation radio communications to improve situational awareness when operating in controlled airspace.

Aeronautical Decision-Making

Learn risk mitigation, Crew Resource Management, and the proper decision-making strategies to keep you safe and ensure mission success.

Physiology

Students will learn how drugs, alcohol, the environment, and other factors affect human physiology and effective strategies to prevent impairment before and during flight.

Day 3

Wire Environment

The wire environment is the most challenging environment in which we fly. Learn the proper techniques and methodologies to ensure safety and effectively capture data.

Crew Coordination

Working with a crew can pose its own challenges.Learn how to effectively assign tasks and roles and how to successfully manage a drone flight crew.

Phraseology

Students will learn a common set of words and phrases to effectively communicate during flight.

Mission Planning

We discuss and demonstrate how to plan and execute a flight from start to finish.

Preflight Inspection

Learn how to correctly inspect your drone and equipment before flight.
